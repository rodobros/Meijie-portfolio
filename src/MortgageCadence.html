Mortgage Cadence

A Loan Origination System (LOS) owned by Accenture. Inside of MC, there’s Collaboration Center. It is a secured platform that connects lenders, 3rd party service providers and everyone related to a mortgage lending process. They can easily exchange messages, manage orders and files all in one place.

Roles: Research, Information Architecture, UX/UI Design, Prototype

Tools: Figma, ADO

Duration: Jan 2022 - Jan 2023

Design Process

CHALLENGE 1

How might we streamline the registration process?

I was not tasked to improve the registration process; however, during my research and heuristic analysis, I identified potential areas for improvement. The current process consisted of seventeen steps with numerous security checks, and the security checks became the ultimate stoppers to clients. In addition, basic plan clients had no direct way to contact support, leading them to reach out to professional plan clients and causing frustration among paying clients.

By closely working with customer service, developers and security teams, I worked diligently to reduce the number of steps from 17 to just 6, while still maintaining necessary security measure.

Before

Upon logging in, users are directed to the dashboard page of the previous product, which featured a flat design with all the features presented on a single page. This lack of hierarchy and organization made it challenging to navigate and utilize the platform effectively.

After

The new design focuses on a more intuitive and organized experience, upon accessing the dashboard, users can now have an overview of their loans and easily navigate to specific loan details. Instead of a flat layout, the new page solves the problem by introducing a tab-based layout. The four tabs are: Messages, Orders, Files and Contacts.

CHALLENGE 2

How might we help users to submit and receive proper orders?

During research, I learnt that It takes 25 exchanges to close an order. Lenders struggle to submit a proper order, and providers are frustrated about the # of times they receive an order with missing documents.

50% of customer support calls are dedicated to addressing orders.

To solve the problem, I analyzed the previous design, and highlighted the main problems when creating an order.

Research

Moving on to pain-point 4: Why is the Attach File section so unintuitive?
Problem 1: Lenders don’t know what documents are needed to submit a proper order.
Decision: Have both required and optional files to reduce the back and forth.

Problem 2: The main problem was related to the "attach and upload" flow. The old design had a strong focus on attaching instead of uploading, so after users uploaded their document, they had to go back to the attach tab and search through the list of files to find the ones they had just uploaded. This unnecessarily slowed down the process and created frustration for sure.
To understand the root cause of these issues, I engaged with different teams to learn about why the old product was designed that way, clients behaviours and the context of uploading a file, etc. While deep diving into this problem, it revealed additional problems related to “File“ tab.

With their pain points in mind, I decided to tackle the “File” area first because it will directly affect the “attach and upload” flow of ordering and messaging. I explored a few ideas, and finally decided to make “Files” a repository place.

With insights from research and a bunch of iterations, below are the decisions made for pain point 4 and the redesign of “Files” tab.

  1. Files: tab: Made it a repository to avoid confusions..
  2. Orders:Added both required files and optional files when placing an order.
  3. Orders: Redesigned “upload and attach” modal to streamline the process, now users can upload and attach right away, the system also provides real time feedback which contributed to the efficiency of the process

These issues collectively resulted in redundant work and wasted time as designers struggled to align their efforts and recreate components that could have been readily available

REFLECTION

I found this project to be highly enjoyable and challenging for several reasons.

  1. The diverse range of users, including power users, beginners, and various stakeholders, added complexity to the design process. The project required careful considerations in order to balance the intuitiveness for all these user.
  2. Working on a legacy product brought unique challenges, requiring creative problem-solving within existing development and security constraints. Balancing innovation, compatibility, and security was a constant consideration throughout the project.
  3. Not having direct access to customers posed a unique challenge. Despite limitations, I proactively conducted research and sought usability testing opportunities.

Overall, this project provided valuable lessons and experiences in working with diverse user groups, addressing legacy system constraints, and conducting research without direct customer access. It highlighted the importance of adaptability, creativity, and a user-centered approach in delivering effective UX solutions.
