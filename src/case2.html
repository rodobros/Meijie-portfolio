La Maison de Soya

La Maison de Soya is a small quick dining restaurant located in the Greater Montreal. They serve traditional Northern Chinese food including soy food and drinks.

Roles: User Research, Information Architecture, UX/UI Design, Prototype, Testing.

Tools: Sketch, InVision, Photoshop

Duration: Oct - Nov 2019

The Challenge

Create an online presence to strengthen its brand value (Healthy, fresh, authentic, affordable, cozy), educate people about their products and ultimately attract more customers.

Proposed Solution


Process

RESEARCH

To understand the industry, trends and target audiences.
After talking to the owner about their business direction and their struggles for attracting more consumers, I started to conduct market research to learn about the industry and the kind of products they offer. The research helped me to determine the customer segment,understand their challenges and the trend.

Market Research

Competitive Analysis

It is important to know how competitors do in order to succeed, I then identified direct (businesses offer the same products) and indirect competitors (businesses offer substitutes products) of La Maison de Soya. The analysis shows that La Maison de Soya is the only business that does not have its own website, which means a strong online presence is needed to compete against other competitors.

Interview

In order to gain more empathy towards users, I conducted interviews with 6 consumers of the restaurant that are aged from 22-35 (3 Females, 3 Males). I aimed to understand how target users discover a new restaurant, their in store and online experiences with La Maison de Soya. During my trip to the restaurant, I also observed consumer behaviors and the atmosphere at the restaurant. By the end, I was able to learn more about the business and their consumers.

DEFINE

After interviews, I created this empathy map to better visualize and organize my findings to uncover users’ needs, goals, motivations and frustrations.

Empathy Map

Insights:

  1. Other customers’ opinions influence users’ restaurants choices.
  2. Users go to restaurants based on friends’ recommendations.
  3. Users go to restaurants based on the taste of the food.

Needs:

  1. To learn about a restaurant through other customers.
  2. To discover new restaurants through friends.
  3. To know if the food suits​ her taste.

Persona

The empathy map helped me to create our persona “Eva”. Eva represents La Maison de Soya’s target consumers as someone who’s always looking for tasty meals to reward herself after a week of hard work.

Product Roadmap

After defining our target users, I created POV statements and HMW question to help brainstorm potential solutions. A Venn diagram was also created to show the shared goals because I wanted to make sure we understand the big picture. Next, I created this product roadmap to prioritize goals, features and metrics for the website and also prioritized them based on their impact and feasibility. This roadmap helped to me to define key features for the website.

Prototype + Testing

With the mid-fidelity wireframes, I conducted 6 usability tests to gain feedback about the layout. Each participant was given specific scenarios with the focus on finding general information about the business, viewing the menu and locating information about how to visit the restaurant. At the end, the synthesized data indicated that there’s no problem in relates to the layout and navigation. There were some users got confused by a few design details that were resolved in the iterations.

Branding

Before moving into high-fidelity wireframes, I created a mood board to gather references for the design, a style tile to make sure my design would be consistent. My role was to help the business to enhance their brand value (Healthy, fresh, tasty, affordable, cozy), so I decided to keep the same logo and same typography approach (serif logo and sans serif menu) because the business has been using it throughout their branding. Colors were chosen based on their brand value and the colors of their products.
