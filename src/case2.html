La Maison de Soya

La Maison de Soya is a small quick dining restaurant located in the Greater Montreal. They serve traditional Northern Chinese tofu dishes, soya milk, and bubble tea. The business underwent a change of ownership in April 2019. The new owner is thinking about to have an online presence to strengthen their brand value, educate people about their products and ultimately attract more people.

Roles: User Research, Information Architecture, UX/UI Design, Prototype, Testing.

Tools: Sketch, InVision, Principle, Photoshop

Duration: June 2019 (2 weeks/80 hours)

Problem Statement

How might we help consumers to learn about La Maison de Soya?

Proposed Solution

Design from scratch a modern, clean and responsive website that enhances the restaurant’s brand value (Healthy, fresh, authentic, affordable, cozy).


Process

EMPATHIZE

Market Research

To understand the industry, trends and target audiences.
After talking to the owner about their business direction and their struggles for attracting more consumers, I started to conduct market research to learn about the industry and the kind of products they offer. The research helped me to determine the customer segment, to understand their challenges and the trend.

Competitive Analysis

It is important to know how competitors do in order to succeed, I then identified direct (businesses offer the same products) and indirect competitors (businesses offer substitutes products) of La Maison de Soya. The analysis shows that La Maison de Soya is the only business that does not have its own website, which means a strong online presence is needed to compete against other competitors.

Interview

In order to gain more empathy towards users, I conducted interviews with 6 consumers of the restaurant that are aged from 22-35. I aimed to understand how target users discover a new restaurant, their in store and online experiences with La Maison de Soya. During my trip to the restaurant, I also observed consumer behaviors and the atmosphere at the restaurant. By the end, I was able to learn more about the business and their consumers.

DEFINE

To clarify details of what will be created, for who and how.
This empathy map was created to visually organize my interview findings and to help understand the mindset of our users. I also looked for patterns to uncover users’ needs, goals, and pains.

Empathy Map

Insights:

  1. Other customers’ opinions influence users’ restaurants choices.
  2. Users go to restaurants based on friends’ recommendations.
  3. Users go to restaurants based on the taste of the food.

Needs:

  1. To learn about a restaurant through other customers.
  2. To discover new restaurants through friends.
  3. To know if the food suits​ her taste.

Persona

The insights from empathy map helped me to create our persona “Eva”. Eva represents La Maison de Soya’s target consumers as someone who’s always looking for tasty meals to reward herself after a week of hard work. By giving personally to the research data, we can better empathize with target users throughout the design process.

POV + HMW

After defining our target users from research, I created point of view statements using insights and needs from previous research. These statements helped to craft “how might we” questions to define user problems and to help brainstorm for solutions.

Product Roadmap

Keeping HMW questions in mind, I started to brainstorm potential solutions. A Venn diagram was also created to show the shared goals because I wanted to make sure we address business goals, user goals and technical considerations. I then created this product roadmap to prioritize goals, features and metrics for the website. I prioritized them based on their impact and feasibility. This roadmap helped to me to define key features for the website.

Sitemap

Next, I created a sitemap based on common patterns of restaurants’ websites. This sitemap shows how information should be organized and structured for users to easily navigate the website.

Information Architecture

Task flow + User flow

After finishing the sitemap, I created a task flow to show the most common flow of our users. Then a user flow to show how our users will interact with the site given different scenarios I found from the research phase. User flow and task flow helped me to effectively identify key screens and the relationship between them.

DESIGN

Low-fidelity Wireframes

This stage is to identify solutions to problems through design.
I started to sketch out low-fidelity wireframes to show the layout and information architecture of the website to solve users problem. These sketches allowed me to get quick feedback at early stage of the design process.

Mid-fidelity Wireframes

Next, I digitized responsive mid-fidelity wireframes based on my sketches to show the layout and the visual hierarchy of the website.

PROTOTYPE + TESTING

Before moving further into the design process, I decided to a usability test to gain feedback about the layout. I created a test plan that outlined the test objectives, methodologies, tasks and participants’ recruit plan. I then conducted 6 in-person usability tests using my prototype. Each participant was given specific scenarios with the focus on finding general information about the business, viewing the menu and locating information about how to visit the restaurant. This allowed me to gain valuable feedback from users.

ITERATION

To iterate upon designs and develop high-fidelity wireframes

Affinity Map

I created an affinity map to better visualize my findings and observations from usability test. This helped me to identify patterns and trends. In the end, the synthesized data indicated that there’s no problem in relates to the layout and navigation. However, some small changes of titles could be made in order to avoid confusions due to the usage of different languages in Quebec.

Branding

Before moving into high-fidelity wireframes, I created a mood board to gather references for the design, a style tile to make sure my design would be consistent. My role was to help the business to enhance their brand value (Healthy, fresh, tasty, affordable, cozy), so I decided to keep the same logo and same typography approach (serif logo and sans serif menu) because the business has been using it throughout their branding. Colors were chosen based on their brand value and the colors of their products.

Style Tile

UI Design

Using the brand style tile I created, I applied stylings, images and typography to my mid-fidelity wireframes. I also created icons that fit with the logo to enhance the cozy feeling. I then made a desktop version prototype with my high-fidelity wireframes which you probably noticed at the begining of the page.

REFLECTION

Overall, this project was relatively straightforward due to the small scale of the business. It was a very rewarding and enjoyable project since I was working with a real business I had to keep in mind both consumers’ and business goals when I was designing. The constraints of the business’s branding pushed me to work harder to make everything fit well in order to covey a consistent message.

What I learned:

We design for both users and stakeholders, do your best to satisfy everyone’s needs. Make everything possible!

What would I have done differently giving more time?

  • I would work with the business to develop a page for online reservation.
  • I would create more pages by learning more about the restaurant’s B2B approach since they also sell their products to local supermarkets.
