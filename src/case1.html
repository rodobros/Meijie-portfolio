Earth

To comply with NDA,  these designs are a reinterpretation of the original using a fictitious brand.

Roles: User research, information architecture, UX/UI Design

Tools: Sketch, Zeplin, Keynote, Google Sheet, Jira

Duration: Jan - Mar 2020

The Challenges

Using existing technology to create the future of e-commerce for Earth to sell to prospective clients. Specifically, an e-commerce app that’s modular enough for any retail company to leverage their current technology and get started on the platform.

The Solution

An application that provides detailed guidance and support to help users shop faster and find products that are best suited for them without hassle. The app will also help users to keep up with the latest trends.

Process

RESEARCH

I began my project by talking to shareholders and learned this project should be focusing on creating an e-commerce app with a focus on fashion retailers. I then did a market research to learn more about current market, e-commerce trends and factors that influence online shopping.

Market Research

Competitive Analysis

Next, I did a competitive analysis on below five popular fashion retailers with a focus on identifying strengths and weaknesses of their current app/website. This allowed me to see how Earth can stand out from the crowd.

Interviews

Once I had a basic understanding of the market, it was time to dive deeper into consumers’ behaviors, needs and pain points. I conducted Interviews with four femles from 20-48 who shop clothes at least once a month. I focused on asking them open-ended questions about their sopping experiences.

DEFINE

With interview notes, I started to synthesizing my findings to uncover our user needs and painpoints, then created our persona Kylie to represent our target market. Kylie is a young, working professional who wants to keep up with the latest trends and find clothes that are best suited for her without hassle.

Persona

Goals

After defining our target users, I created below Venn diagram to show the shared goals because I wanted to make sure I address everyone's needs while designing. .

Feature Roadmap

Then me and other designers started to brainstorm possible features based on past experience and user goals. We came up with a list of features and I prioritized them by feasibility, impact and level of difficulty.

Sitemap

Being an online fashion platform with a massive choice of products, as such, a clearly organized sitemap was crucial to visualize the high-level content. After analyzing design patterns for other e-commerce apps, I created a sitemap to layout information in a way our users would expect.

DESIGN

With the help of sitemap, I started to design solutions for our users. During the design process, I had meetings with other designers at least once a week to get feedback. Design deliverables included creating design system to increase efficiency and also to make sure my design would be consistent. While designing, I also created some task flows and User flows to identify relationships between screens. Having an agile work environment was very beneficial since we kept everyone informed about the latest changes.

REFLECTION

Designing an e-commerce app might seem to be straightforward since it is a mature market with a lot of existing design patterns; however, there are still aspects being neglected which greatly impact sales. If we want to stand out from the crowd, we need to really understand our customers, keep learning about the latest technologies and trends. As a designer, we should always strive to find what the real problem is and what problem is worth solving for.
