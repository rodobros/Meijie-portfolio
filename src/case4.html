TrueSkin

An end-to-end skincare database app aims to help everyone find the right products.

Roles: User Research, Information Architecture, UX/UI Design, Prototype, Testing.

Tools: Sketch, InVision.

The Challenge

Buying skincare products is a trial and error process. There are so many products available on the market, we waste a lot of money because we struggle to understand products.

The Solution

Trueskin app provides an unbiased database of skincare products. It leverages AI algorithms to give personalized products recommendations by learning about user skin types and past purchases. The app helps everyone to become skincare experts, avoid wasting money and find the right products.


Process

RESEARCH

To understand the industry, trends and target audiences.
I started off by doing online market research to learn more about current market trends, target consumers and their shopping behaviors. The research showed me the importance of this topic and nearly half women consumers research ingredients while shopping for skincare products.

Market Research

Competitive Analysis

After researching many different apps/websites, I decided to focus on below four competitors because they offer a variety of different ways for users to find skincare products information or ingredients. Through this analysis, I learned what features are common in skincare-focused apps.

Interview

To learn more about our target users, I conducted 5 in-person interviews with individuals that are interested in skincare. Participants are all females, aged from 26-38. For the interviews, I focused on asking consumers’ experiences with skincare including their skincare routines, skincare products, how they shop for skincare, how often they try new products and their knowledge of skincare ingredients.

DEFINE

To clarify details of what will be created, for who and how.
After 5 interviews, I began synthesizing my findings by organizing all data into an empathy map to uncover our users’ needs, motivation, goals and frustrations. I found that our users' buying decisions are heavily influenced by social media and friends, they want to understand ingredients to make sure they find products that work for their skin, but they struggle to understand products ingredients.

Empathy Map

Insights:

  1. Users are influenced by other people.
  2. Users check ingredients before purchasing.
  3. Users want to make sure products work before purchasing.

Needs:

  1. To learn about products through other people.
  2. To understand products’ ingredients.
  3. To find products that match their skin types .

Persona

The above empathy map helped me to create our persona “Chloé”. Chloé represents the millennial target consumers of TrueSkin. She’s a young work professional who wants to find products that work for her skin, but struggles to understand products.

POV + HMW

After defining our target users, I created point of view statements for our users using insights and needs from previous research. These statements helped me to created “how might we” questions to define user problems and to help brainstorm for solutions.

Product Roadmap

Using the HMW questions, I started to brainstorm potential solutions. A Venn diagram was also created to show the shared goals because I wanted to make sure I understand the big picture. I then created this product roadmap to prioritize goals and features for the app. I prioritized them based on their impact and feasibility. This roadmap helped to me to define key features for the app.

Sitemap

Next, I checked out other similar apps to analyze their layouts and created this sitemap to represent the information architecture. While creating the sitemap, I made sure I refer to users’ needs to organize information in a way that users would expect.

Information Architecture

Task flow + User flow

After finishing the sitemap, I created a task flow to show one of the most common flows of our users. Then a user flow to show various ways that our users could interact with our app based on their decisions and different scenarios I found during the research phase. Task flow and user flow helped me to effectively identify key screens and the relationship between them.

DESIGN

This stage is to identify solutions to problems through design.
I started the process of UI design with sketching. I sketched out key screens with the user flows I’d mapped out. This helped me to think though the layout, interaction and flow of these screens before moving into digital wireframes. I was able to get quick feedback at the early stage of design.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

Mid-fidelity Wireframes

After collecting feedback from other designers, I started to digitize my sketches. As I was designing, I made sure to reference Apple’s IOS Human guidelines and other common designs patterns to ensure leveraging of users’ familiarity with elements to create an intuitive experience.

PROTOTYPE + TESTING

With the Mid-fidelity wireframes, I created a mobile prototype using InVision and condected 5 usability tests to get usability data from target consumers. Participants were given specific scenarios with the focus on exploring the app's navigation while interacting with key features.

Affinity Map

I created an affinity map to better visualize my findings and observations from usability tests. This helped me to identify patterns and organize feedback in a way that was easier to extract design recommendations from.

Insights:

  1. 80% users didn’t know how many products they could compare.
  2. 60% users would like to go back to home screen instantly.
  3. 60% users didn’t understand numbers under fragrance/preservative.
  4. 40% users would like to learn more about what ingredients to avoid.

Design Recommendations:

  1. Show thumbnails of checked products to tell users which product they’ve selected and how many they can select.
  2. Add tab bar to all screens.
  3. Show “2 preservatives" instead of just a number.
  4. Make “2 preservatives” a link to provide detailed info.

Branding

Before moving into high-fidelity wireframes, I wanted to establish a clear visual direction for TrueSkin’s branding. I first defined the brand attributes (trustworthy, approachable, modern, simple, openness) and created a mood board to gather references. I then worked on some explorations of TrueSkin’s logo. I ultimately decided to use blue/purplish wordmark with a water drop to represents “be true to your skin” mission.

REFLECTION

From resistant to somewhat sensitive skin because I tried so many different products that didn’t work for my skin. It was very exciting to work on this project because I want to thelp everyone find the right products.

What I learned:

  • Define problems with research. Skincare is such a huge market. I was overwhelmed by all the different angles I could approach it from. Research helped me to know what to focus on.
  • Know how to prioritize. As someone who’s passionate about this topic, I wanted to add a lot of features for the app. This made the app look overwhelmed with information. I had to take a step back and focus on the features that users need the most.
