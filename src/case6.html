Lowe’s Fidelity Program

Lowe’s approached us to help them launching their Contractor rewards fidelity program (Now known as VIPpro). I redesigned and updated some parts of their customer app for iOS and Android, employee web and customer web.
Below case study mainly focuses on how I successfully solved key usability issues and integrated RONA and Réno-Dépot member cards seamlessly to the app.

CHALLENGE 3

Before vs After: How might we help Mike to manage purchase histories?

Purchase History Flow

It’s the time that Mike needs to track his personal and business spending. With the old app, Mike basically cannot find any useful information because of the unintuitive flow without any filters.

Due to constraints, we couldn’t add filters. In order to make sure Mike can manage purchase histories easily, we did a lot of brainstorming sessions trying to figure out the flow. Should personal and business purchase histories be separated or consolidated in one place? How should we do it without filters? How should we present information? Finally, with whiteboard sessions and many iterations, we decided to centralize business and personal spending in one place so that Mike doesn’t need to go back and forth when viewing purchase histories.

PROTOTYPE

REFLECTION

This project was harder than I anticipated since there are many different user (Member & Manager etc) and limitations. I really enjoyed how it challenged me as a designer. Even though I tried to build my products from users’ perspective, I came up with great ideas, I wanted to implement advanced search, sort, filter functions, but in reality, sometimes we just couldn’t do it. It might be because the company does not have the resource to do it or the business’s approach could not be changed just like that etc. The product didn’t have all the features I hoped for, but I was happy that I managed to build a intuitive product with lots of restrictions.
