Bank of Montreal

Effortlessly managing finances with BMO's new tracking and budgeting features.

Roles: User Research, Information Architecture, UX/UI Design, Prototype, Testing.

Tools: Sketch, InVision, Principle

The Challenge

BMO’s current app offers very basic functions, to stay competitive, BMO should understand its users and provide more advanced features that users truly need. Research shows millennials are actively delaying having children due to debts. Money budgeting is important, but people find current budgeting tools (excel and apps) are either too complicated, too time consuming or does not provide accurate data. The challenge is to make BMO’s new money budgeting and tracking feature as simple and accurate as possible for both novice and advanced users.

The Solution

Integrate a money budgeting and tracking feature into BMO’s mobile app seamlessly.The new feature automatically calculates monthly budget and categories transactions based on user behaviors. Users can also personalize categories for each transaction to make money managing more accurately.


Process

RESEARCH

To understand the industry, trends and target audiences.
I started off by doing online market research to learn more about this topic. My goals were to understand more about mobile banking industry including their trends and challenges. How consumers currently budget and tracking their money. The research shows that bank apps should target millennials and generation X. In additional, nearly half of millennials want their bank to provide tools to help them monitor budget.

Market Research

Competitive Analysis

I decided to conduct a heuristic evaluation with BMO’s current mobile app to give me a better understanding of their design patterns. Next, I did a competitive analysis on the Big Five banks of Canada.This analysis helps me to know how BMO positions itself against competitors. In general, BMO is doing well in terms of usability, but their app is very limited in functionality as compared to all other banks. It would be advantageous for BMO to offer more features that millennials want. .

Interview

To learn more about our target users, I conducted 6 in-person interviews with BMO’s consumers that are aged from 20-35. (4 Females, 2 Males) All participants have or had money budgeting and tracking habit. For the interviews, I focused on asking consumers’ experiences with BMO both online and in branch, their experiences with money budgeting/Tracking.

DEFINE

To clarify details of what will be created, for who and how.
After 6 interviews, I began synthesizing my findings by organizing all data into an empathy map to uncover our users’ needs, motivation, goals and frustrations. This empathy map was very useful to visualize our findings and helped me to understand the mindset of our users.

Empathy Map

Insights:

  1. Users want to save time.
  2. Users track their spending.
  3. Users budget their money.

Needs:

  1. To have simple to use applications.
  2. To know their financial status.
  3. To feel in control of their money.

Persona

The above empathy map helped me to create our persona “Maya”. Maya represents the millennial target consumers of BMO. She’s a young work professional who needs to manage her money carefully because she wants to save money for big purchases, but she finds budgeting too difficult.

Define problem

After defining our target users, I created POV statements and HMW questions to define user problems. I then created a Venn diagram to show the shared goals because I wanted to make sure I address everyone's needs. Next a roadmap was created to prioritize goals and features based on their impacts and feasibility. This roadmap helped me to define key features.

Ideate solutions

Sitemap

I wanted to make sure the new features will be seamlessly integrated in the app for our users to easily navigate. I went to my BMO app to analyze their layout and created a sitemap to visually represent the information architecture. While creating the sitemap, I made sure I refer to users’ needs to organize information in a way that users would expect.

Information Architecture

User flow

After finishing the sitemap, I created a user flow to show how our users could interact with our app based on their decisions and different scenarios I found during the research phase.

DESIGN

This stage is to identify solutions to problems through design.
I started to sketch out low-fidelity wireframes to show the layout and information architecture of the new screens. I also consistently checked my BMO app to make sure my design would be consistent with their design pattern. This helped me to quickly and efficiently get feedback before deciding on which direction to move on.

PROTOTYPE + TESTING

With the high-fidelity wireframes, I created a mobile prototype using InVision and conducted 5 in-person usability tests with strangers. The test results showed that all users were able to accomplish the tasks, but had some troubles with few design details that were resolved in the iterations.

REFLECTION

I can't stress enough how important research and usability testings are. I started the project with a vague idea of implementing a budgeting and money tracking feature for BMO's customers without knowing much about budgeting. As research progressed, our user goals and pain points started to be clear, I knew what problems I should be solving for users. As designers we need to talk to our users and really understand them. Plus, don't forget about usability testing, we designers sometimes take things for granted, we don’t see little problems, but users are different, by testing with them, we can better understand their pains.

What would I have done differently giving more time?

  • I'd like to develop a section that helps users to save money to reach their goals.
