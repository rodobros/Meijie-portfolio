OnMo

OnMo is a social and centralized gaming platform designed for everyone to watch or play only the highlights of popular mobile games without downloading a single one. Below case studies shows an overview of my process and some screens. For more details, please contact me.

Roles: Research, Information Architecture, UX/UI Design, Design System, Illustration, branding.

Tools: Figma, Jira

Duration: Nov 2020 - Apr 2021

My Role

After rob0 was released, the investor company OnMobile really liked it. Since then, I’ve been leading the design of OnMo gaming platfrom from scratch, getting involved in feature planning, user flow, and defining the branding direction with stakeholders. I also created the internal CMS tool which leverages machine learning for develpers to extract metada from games to create content for the gaming platform.
Below case study focuses on the MVP of the gaming platform.

Process

The Challenges

Due to the loneliness of quarantine, people were getting into gaming to stay connected and occupied. Help OnMo to create a centralized platform that helps people with busy schedules to socialize and transition into the gaming community.

The Solution

I’ve used all the insights from research to design the mobile gaming platform. With OnMobile’s MVP version, you can easily try different games with in-depth game discovery. You can have a competitive experience by beating high scores, and participatie in asychronous battles.

REFLECTION

The gaming platform was such an interesting and rewarding project since I was the only designer and had to wear lots of hats. With this project, I communicated closely with everyone to plan features, timelines etc. and I also got the chance to really dive deeper into the business side of product development. Below are some of the challenges I encourterd, my success and what I learnt.

Challenges:

  • Balance needs from many different stakeholders.
  • Tight deadlines led to compromised UX process./li>
Success:
  • Released Alpha versions within tight deadlines.
  • Each version had an incremental customer satisfaction.
What I learnt:
  • Have a growth mindset and to think of monetization when developing new features.
  • As a sole UX designer, it is important to educate colleagues about UX process.
